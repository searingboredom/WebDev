The Brown Bear

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia and North America. Its conservation status is currently "Least Concern." There are many subspecies within the brown bear species, including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Learn more about Brown Bears

Brown bears are omnivores, with their diet varying widely depending on habitat and season, including berries, roots, fish, and small mammals.

Brown bears are known for their impressive hibernation periods, which can last up to 7 months in some regions.

Here are some bear species

  • Arctos
  • Collarus
  • Horribilis
  • Nelsoni (extinct)

The following countries have the largest population of brown bears:

  1. Russia
  2. United States
  3. Canada

Conservation Efforts

Despite their "Least Concern" status, many organizations are working to protect brown bear habitats and promote coexistence with human populations.
